From scattered conditions to the export commercial master source

José Carlos Garrigós · Nineteenth collaborative contribution to the hub on centralising prices, commercial conditions and ERP-connected export operations

The problem

In export operations, price and commercial-condition information is spread across tariffs from different years, discounts, negotiated net prices, special family conditions and lists in multiple currencies. The difficulty is not the absence of information, but the lack of a centralised source clearly connected to daily operations.

The proposal is not only about checking a price faster. It is about turning the real export commercial policy into a live, traceable master source that can be used both by the team and by the ERP.

What this initiative proposes

The proposal defines a single, editable and easily searchable tool containing each customer’s commercial conditions: tariff discounts, individual net prices, validity periods, product-family rules and agreed currencies.

This base would not only serve as a consultation tool for the commercial and export team. It should also feed the ERP automatically with the valid condition in force, reducing repetitive manual entry, errors and dependence on individual tacit knowledge.

What it would bring to the business

Reliability

More certainty when identifying the correct price by customer, product and currency without relying on scattered historical files.

Speed

Faster response to customers, offers and order validation through one live commercial reference.

Traceability

Clear visibility of which commercial condition applies, since when, under what logic and for which customer or family.

Scalability

A foundation ready for future automation, tariff reviews and commercial analysis by customer, product, market or currency.

Key learnings

  • Commercial dispersion is not solved by adding more documents, but by establishing one well-governed master source.
  • The main risk today is not missing data, but competing versions that are partial, outdated or not connected to each other.
  • Automation into the ERP only makes sense when there is first a reliable and centralised commercial logic.
  • A strong pricing tool does more than improve lookup speed: it turns the real commercial policy into something operational.
Collaborative conclusion: José Carlos Garrigós’ proposal transforms export price and condition management into a single consultation and ERP-feeding base designed to improve speed, coherence and traceability around a critical commercial dataset.
