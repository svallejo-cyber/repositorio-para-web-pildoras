Need for a centralised tool for export prices and commercial conditions

Starting point

Today, in the export department, customer price information is spread across different supports and sources: tariffs from different years, commercial discounts, individually negotiated net prices, special product or product-line conditions, and lists in different currencies, mainly EUR and USD.

This dispersion makes it difficult to identify quickly and reliably which is the correct price to apply to a given customer for a given product. The problem is not missing information, but the fact that the information is not centralised, standardised, updated or clearly connected to daily operations.

Current operating risk

The ERP currently holds a price or commercial-condition data point that must be entered manually. This creates additional administrative burden and, above all, a meaningful risk of errors, inconsistencies, outdated data and dependence on the knowledge of specific people within the team.

When commercial information is split across multiple files or historical records, the team loses agility in answering customers, preparing offers, reviewing transactions or validating orders.

Proposal

The need identified is for a single, editable and easily searchable tool that centrally captures all commercial conditions for each customer. This tool should integrate tariff discounts, individual net prices, conditions by family or product line, validity periods and agreed currencies, so that all relevant information is available in one live and updated environment.

Connection to the ERP

Besides serving as a consultation tool for the commercial and export department, this base should be connectable to the ERP so the current valid information can be uploaded automatically. This would avoid repetitive manual entry and ensure stronger coherence between the agreed commercial condition and the operational data used in the system.

Expected value

The value of this tool would be twofold. On one side, it would allow the commercial team to access a clear and up-to-date view of the price of any product for any customer in the relevant currency. On the other, it would turn that same information into a reliable base to feed the ERP, improving data quality and reducing administrative incidents.

In practical terms, a solution of this kind would organise and give traceability to the real export commercial policy, preventing it from being split across historical documents, individual files or unstructured knowledge. It would also facilitate future automations, price reviews, tariff updates and commercial analysis by customer, product, market or currency.

Conclusion

The need is to have a centralised instrument for managing prices and commercial conditions that works at the same time as a consultation tool and as the master information source for the ERP. The final objective is to gain reliability, speed, traceability and management capacity around information that is critical to the daily activity of the export department.
