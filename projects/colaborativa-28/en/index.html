From rebuilding the model to scaling it
Spain–Portugal sales consolidation without duplicating measures

Francisco Gomis Bayona · Twenty-eighth collaborative contribution to the hub on Power BI consolidation, model scalability and Spain-Portugal reporting

The problem

For the 2026 budgeting process, sales needed to be analyzed in a consolidated Spain–Portugal view, but the existing model only allowed each company to be reviewed separately.

The challenge was not just to add two realities together, but to do it without internal duplication and without rewriting nearly 200 measures already used across reporting.

A manual approach would have meant days or weeks of work, intensive testing and a high risk of breaking the model.

What has been built

Paco introduced an extra logic layer through Tabular Editor so users can switch between Spain, Portugal and Consolidated from one selection point.

The consolidated logic adds both companies while excluding Spain sales made to Portugal’s internal customer, which removes duplication.

What changes in the model

Consolidation affects around 200 measures transversally without duplicating them.

Existing reports continue to work without redesign.

Users can move between company view and consolidated view without changing report structure.

Business impact

Fast response to a real budgeting and analysis need.

Drastic time saving: a task that could have taken weeks was solved in roughly three hours.

More reliable consolidated data and simpler maintenance going forward.
