Collaborative pill · Isaval team

From dock congestion to planned logistics slots

Marcos Villaverde Fontán · New collaborative contribution on dock scheduling, slot windows and better load and unload balance

The problem

Logistics operations currently suffer from highly concentrated peaks, especially between 17:00 and 19:00 for agency loads. This is compounded by bulky inbound deliveries that can block preparation areas when several coincide on the same day.

The proposal is not just another calendar. It is a shared planning system for docks, with advance reservation, capacity visibility and less daily operational friction.

What this initiative proposes

Marcos proposes a dock scheduling app, accessible internally and externally, allowing loading or unloading slots to be booked in available time windows. Depending on the supplier and operation type, the system would automatically assign the required dock occupation time.

The core logic is simple: request reservation 24 hours in advance so teams can prepare resources and orders, reorganise exceptions and avoid avoidable last-minute overloads.

What it would bring to the business

Order

One source of truth for available slots, less overlap and better distribution of inbound and outbound traffic.

Efficiency

Less manual coordination effort by the logistics team and fewer calls or emails to schedule operations.

Preparation

Ability to assign staff, prepare goods and reduce truck dwell time before arrival.

Data

Traceability of punctuality, real operation duration, peak hours and saturation points for continuous improvement.

Key learnings

This is not only a technology tool. It is an operational model change toward smarter planning, anticipation and more supplier autonomy inside a controlled framework.

The proposal also opens the door to automations such as confirmations, reminders and alerts for delays or incidents.

Collaborative conclusion: Marcos Villaverde’s proposal turns dock management into an active planning layer with advance reservation, better work distribution and an operational dataset that helps improve continuously.
