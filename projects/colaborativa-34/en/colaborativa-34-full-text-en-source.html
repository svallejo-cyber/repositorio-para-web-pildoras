Living onboarding and internal mobility system

Objective: turn onboarding into a complete, consistent and accessible process that serves both new hires and internal promotions, avoiding information loss and repeated explanation.

The key is not to improve a welcome session. The key is to create a common living base that remains useful after day one.

Starting point

Every incorporation triggers the same need: explain how the company works, how work is organized, which rules apply, who the relevant contacts are and what is expected from the role. Part of that information is shared in sessions, part in emails and part through the manager or the team.

Over time, an important share becomes harder to access, gets scattered across one-off communications or turns outdated. That creates friction, repetition and inconsistency for HR, managers and employees.

What changes in the approach

The proposal treats onboarding as a full process. The person not only receives information but knows where to find it later through a single organized source that remains available over time.

This same structure also helps with internal promotions and role changes, where the need to explain new concepts, workflows and relationships appears again.

How it is structured

  • Structured common base: key company content, rules, processes and tools organized by topic and available in digital form.
  • Real human support: the manager and the team complete integration through context, relationships, priorities and practical ways of working.
  • Always-available space: repository in Sebastián / Isaval&You where information stays accessible and up to date.

Where the AI layer appears

Once the knowledge base is structured, a faster access layer can be built through an internal assistant or chatbot. That layer would answer recurring questions using the same single source of truth.

  • It does not replace human onboarding.
  • It does improve agility and autonomy.
  • It does reduce dependence on searching emails or asking the same people repeatedly.

Business value

  • Faster, safer integration.
  • More organizational consistency in messages, rules and criteria.
  • Less repeated explanation and better use of time.
  • Useful support in sensitive processes such as attendance, payroll, leave and prevention.
  • Scalability for new hires and internal mobility.
Conclusion: onboarding stops being a one-off moment and becomes a lightweight infrastructure for integration, consultation and continuity that organizes information, gives clarity from the start and prepares a genuinely useful future AI layer.
