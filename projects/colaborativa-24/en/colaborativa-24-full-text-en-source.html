Specification monitoring program for quality control

Context

Quality control has long worked with defined specifications for each product, but there was no tool capable of contrasting those ranges against the actual reality of historical production.

Sergio’s proposal starts from a simple premise: use five years of manufacturing history to understand how critical properties really behave and review whether the current ranges still make sense.

What has been developed

The application is built in Python. It consolidates quality history from Excel and CSV files, cleans and normalizes production data and analyzes each combination of product, color and property.

The system works with properties such as density, viscosity, tinting strength and pH. From there it proposes acceptance ranges based on median ± 20%, compares the current specification against the suggested one and generates exportable technical recommendations.

Architecture and flow

  • Incremental ingestion with traceability of already processed files.
  • Robust cleaning through configurable column aliases.
  • Analytics by product + color + property combination.
  • Recommendation engine with parameterized business rules.
  • Streamlit web interface with visualization and Excel, CSV and PDF exports.

Relevant findings from the project log

The log confirms that the workflow is operational end to end and currently contains 6,179 consolidated rows. It also highlights a meaningful signal: 1,584 viscosity values fall outside the configured range, although they are not automatically excluded today.

That makes the tool more than an analyzer. It becomes a practical basis for deciding whether ranges should be recentered, limits corrected or formulations reviewed.

Business value

  • Technical review grounded in real historical data rather than only theoretical judgement.
  • Potential reduction of adjustments and smoother production flow.
  • Greater ability to detect poorly centered properties or specifications that no longer match actual manufacturing reality.
  • A tool already running and being used, not just a proposal.

Conclusion

The proposal turns quality-control history into an operational basis for reviewing specifications with evidence. Its value lies not only in analysis, but in helping decide when to keep a range, when to adjust it and when a formulation itself should be reviewed to gain stability on the plant floor.
