From trade mission to smart market prospecting

Starting point

Isaval's international activity has shown a special ability to grow in developing, bureaucratically complex or relatively closed markets, where local offer often concentrates on basic and less technical ranges and where commercial and technical support creates real differentiation.

However, the detection of new opportunities still depends to a large extent on trade missions, inbound web contacts, previous relationships and internal judgment. That approach works, but it does not always make it possible to prioritize countries on a homogeneous, comparable and sufficiently data-backed basis.

The challenge is not to make more trips. It is to travel better, after having carried out much deeper and more comparable remote analysis beforehand.

The problem to solve

Today there is no common tool that translates accumulated international experience into an objective and repeatable methodology to decide which markets deserve time, resources and travel.

Country selection, competitor analysis and the identification of potential clients or partners are carried out usefully, but very manually, with uneven depth and without a stable comparative system. This may lead to effort being invested in markets with lower real fit or to travel being decided before remote prospecting has been filtered rigorously enough.

Proposed initiative

The proposal is to develop a market-intelligence and commercial-prospecting tool capable of selecting countries with characteristics similar to those international markets where Isaval already performs well, analyzing competitors, recommending the most suitable entry channel and identifying potential importers, distributors, customers and key actors to activate a back-office prospecting phase before deciding on physical trips.

As a complement, the tool should feed a CRM-like database where all activity is recorded and where commercial memory can be preserved over time.

What it should deliver

  • Better target-country selection and stronger commercial focus.
  • More objectivity and less dependence on intuition as the only starting point.
  • Reduced time and cost in immature prospecting efforts.
  • Better use of commercial back office before traveling.
  • Higher quality in the preparation of meetings and mission agendas.
  • Traceability on why a market is prioritized, postponed or discarded.
  • Preservation of commercial knowledge even when team members change.
  • Better onboarding of future hires through historic context by market.

How it could be implemented

A reasonable path would be phased: first define Isaval's success pattern from its current markets and the implantation models that have worked best; then build a comparative market-analysis model and an evaluation criteria framework; later integrate a commercial prospecting layer to identify key actors and prepare back-office actions; and finally connect all of this to a CRM base that preserves activity, learning and decisions.

Final idea

The initiative does not seek to replace commercial judgment. It seeks to turn accumulated international experience into a more objective, more replicable and more useful methodology to decide where to enter, who to contact first and when a trip actually makes sense. Its value will not lie only in detecting new opportunities, but also in not losing what has already been learned when a market is analyzed, discarded or left under observation.
