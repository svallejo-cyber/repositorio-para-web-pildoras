Tintometric equipment maintenance control: from manual forms to structured management

Lenin Cabrera · Thirty-sixth collaborative contribution to the hub on structured tintometric maintenance control, failure prevention, and the evolution from paper forms to digital management.

The problem

Tintometric equipment maintenance was often handled through isolated printed forms or informal routines, without a clear history, without continuous follow-up, and with heavy dependence on each operator’s judgment.

Lenin Cabrera proposes a structured daily, monthly, quarterly, semiannual, and annual maintenance system for tintometric equipment, restoring traceability, prevention, and a base for future digitization.

What this initiative proposes

The piece proposes a frequency-based control system with technical checklist, owner, execution date, observations, and status, designed to start with standardized forms and later evolve into a centralized digital platform.

Business value

CONTROL

It defines a clear maintenance structure by equipment and by frequency.

TRACEABILITY

It creates a history of interventions, states, and pending actions.

PREVENTION

It reduces operational failures and unexpected stoppages through periodic control.

AUDITABILITY

It provides documentary support for internal processes and certifications.

Collaborative conclusion: The proposal turns tintometric equipment maintenance into a controlled and repeatable process. It may start on paper, but it is already structured enough to be digitized and scaled without losing technical discipline.
