Pill 3. DATA

After a few days of a certain buzz - partly driven by myself, I admit it and even share it with humor - some interesting reactions are starting to appear across the organization.

On one side, an IT department working flat out, practically amazed every day by what they are discovering. Their work horizon is expanding exponentially. They are almost running out of places to store so many new code repositories, and they are starting to realize that the challenge is not only to create, but to master everything that is opening up in front of us.

At the executive committee level, different profiles are also visible. Some early adopters have appeared, others watch with curiosity but prefer to wait, some feel a certain concern (“let someone teach me first”), and there are also people who, for now, remain rather detached from the phenomenon of these new autonomous coding agents.

At a second layer of the organization, a few people are starting to join the wave - Gomis is a good example - others are waiting for their moment... and, in general, not much more.

So far, everything is within expectations.

But we must keep moving. Because in AI terms, January-February 2026 already feels far away. I remain convinced that something very big is going to happen in business and social environments. When it happens, it must find us ready. Not just to ride the wave with dignity... but to surf it better and faster than our competitors.

And this affects everything:

internally, in terms of efficiency, speed, security and profitability;

externally, in our ability to anticipate the market.

And we need to do it now.

That said, I also do not want this first stage - where I am acting more as an ambassador or agitator of the topic - to blur the objective. I am detecting some misinterpretations that should be clarified.

First.

This is not magic. It is not simply a stronger or easier version than ChatGPT to create spectacular videos or very cool things.

This is about transforming how we work.

Second.

Nobody wants you to become programmers.

This is about understanding our business model well and identifying all those things that have always bothered us: slow processes, repetitive tasks, frictions we would like to automate.

This is about learning how to talk to the programmer.

Only now that programmer is called Codex or any other agent.

And it is also about starting to empathize with what until now seemed almost magical and always somewhat opaque: the work of our programmers, internal or external.

The logic will be this: someone detects an opportunity, finds a route to generate an output that improves us, and then IT refines it, integrates it and scales it for everyone.

Because almost always the same thing happens:

your problem is not unique. Another colleague has a similar one. Or your idea triggers a better one.

That is where something very interesting appears: improvements stop being arithmetic and become geometric.

Third.

This is not just another of Santi’s eccentricities.

This is not like the electric car debate - “I will wait until the very end, just in case.”

This is a technological steamroller. Anyone who wants to build a serious professional career cannot afford to turn their back on it.

Fourth.

This is not about “someone teach me” or “where do I start?” either.

This is not like English lessons, where one can get by because a teacher comes to class.

Here we need some lone-coyote spirit.

Explore.

Use the endless resources that exist today to learn.

Run a first pilot.

Make mistakes.

Start again.

Use a coffee break to steal five minutes from someone in IT.

What will not work is taking Luis or Felipe hostage so they become each department’s personal programmer.

And now we come to the most important point of all.

DATA.

Without correct, collected, curated, refined and carefully treated data... there is absolutely nothing we can do.

At Isaval we have been pioneers in many things - as my father reminded us in that video - and one of them was digitization. But it is also true that we have been quite careless with the data generated by that digitization.

Luis was always there to patch things up.

That is over.

AI needs good data.

And it will need more and more data.

Huge volumes.

For Isaval, data must become what TiO2 is for our paints: a critical, top-quality raw material. Without that “pure white,” we will not be able to build anything relevant.

If we do not understand it this way, as Trump would say when talking about Sánchez... we will be losers.

And allow me one final comparison.

Data for Isaval should be like the outfit, tie and clean shave for RNB. Those who came with me on that visit know exactly what I mean.

No more being sloppy with data.

We have to care for it, organize it and respect it.

And on this point, it is worth listening to those who have silently suffered with this for years: Luis, Felipe, Gomis... they know very well what we are talking about.
