From chasing data to managing action: Centralized and automated store alerts

Context

Store operations contain multiple control points, from unsupported expenses and stock incidents to pending orders, missing transfer receipts and open inventories.

With more than 70 stores and an expanding network, the alert volume has made homogeneous manual control increasingly difficult.

Identified problem

The issue was not missing information, but an unscalable management model.

The controller reviewed each incident manually, identified problems case by case and contacted stores individually, often through dozens of calls or highly repetitive emails.

What has been built

A unified alert table now centralizes different business rules and incident types into a single structure.

Each alert includes the store, a clear description of the issue and the concrete action to be taken.

Role of AI and automation

AI helped structure business rules, optimize the logic and accelerate validation cycles.

Power Automate then turned the model into an automatic communication flow for stores and regional managers.

Operational result and conclusion

The model has been working since July 2025 and allows centralized review, automatic communication and more time for action.

The real change is conceptual: the controller no longer chases the data and instead manages the action directly.
