From scattered non-payment handling to a single traceable case file

Mª Ángeles López · Eighteenth collaborative contribution to the hub on structured non-payment handling, automatic file opening and full traceability of actions

The problem

When a customer enters a non-payment situation or exceeds expected payment terms, a significant share of the actions ends up being managed outside the usual company systems: emails, calls, loose documents or one-off interactions with third parties. That makes follow-up harder and disperses critical information.

The proposal is not only about registering a debt. It is about creating a single case file that concentrates information, documents, decisions and actions so every case can be managed with judgement and continuity.

What this initiative proposes

The central element is the non-payment case file: a single space where everything needed to analyse the situation, decide the right path and leave full traceability of what has been done is recorded.

The project also includes an agent able to interpret the information received from the bank, automatically identify the affected customer, linked remittance and invoices associated with the non-payment, and open the file with the initial information already prepared for review and management.

What it would bring to the business

Speed

Faster detection and opening of case files from bank information or unjustified payment overruns.

Order

Centralisation of invoices, delivery notes, bank returns, communications and evidence in a single file.

Traceability

Clear record of what was done, who did it, when and what the next planned step is.

Continuity

Any authorised person can understand the case status and continue the handling without rebuilding the history.

Key learnings

  • The value of the case file is not only in storing documents, but in structuring actions that today remain outside the company’s natural system.
  • A well-focused agent can remove a large amount of manual work in the initial identification and preparation of the case.
  • Non-payment handling gains quality when it is professionalised as a shared process, not as a set of isolated actions.
  • Full traceability is as important as the initial speed of file opening.
Collaborative conclusion: Mª Ángeles López’s proposal turns non-payment handling into a more professional, orderly and auditable flow, supported by a single case file, initial automation and real information continuity.
