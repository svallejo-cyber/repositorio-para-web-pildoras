From invoice to control criteria: a useful review of transport tariffs

Juan Carlos Nuñez · Fourth collaborative contribution to the hub on cost control, tariff review and scalable validation criteria

Context

The project starts from a very concrete operational problem: transport invoices contain the information needed to review costs, but not in a format that allows homogeneous, fast and management-useful validation.

The real leap is not checking invoices faster, but turning their review into a more structured, traceable and scalable control criterion.

What has been designed

The tool compares each invoice against the tariff logic that should apply: shipments, destinations, weights, surcharges, agreed conditions and other specific factors that alter the final result.

The objective is not only to detect whether an invoice looks correct, but to locate relevant differences, prioritize which cases deserve review and reduce the mechanical work that currently consumes expert time.

What it adds to the business

Useful validation

It compares invoiced amounts with the expected tariff and flags discrepancies that genuinely deserve attention.

Scalability

It reduces dependence on a few people with deep tariff knowledge and makes review more homogeneous.

Focus

It shifts effort away from manually checking the obvious and toward interpreting relevant incidents and deviations.

Cost control

It turns invoice review into a clearer, more traceable process connected to economic judgment.

Main lesson

  • Every useful improvement starts from a real day-to-day need, not from abstract digitalization.
  • The value lies in professionalizing a necessary task in a more homogeneous and wider-reaching way.
  • Speed only adds value when it strengthens review judgment rather than replacing it.
Collaborative conclusion: this tool does not replace professional judgment; it prepares it better to detect relevant discrepancies, sustain more consistent control and review transport tariffs with greater analytical capacity.
