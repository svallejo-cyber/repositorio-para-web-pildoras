From blocked invoices to a traceable resolution flow: Structured supplier-issue management to reduce blocks, delays and manual review in Finance

Context

Daily supplier invoice work in Finance gets blocked when there are receiving issues, price differences, wrongly entered orders or invoices without warehouse entry.

Today, resolution requires checking invoices one by one, matching lines, taking screenshots and forwarding the case by email to the area that must correct it.

Problem identified

The issue does not enter a formal workflow. The invoice remains in a pending folder, unposted, absent from forecasts and delaying processes such as promissory note issuance.

The review is repeated every day because there is no single traceable status for the case nor a consolidated view of how long it has been blocked.

What is proposed

Create a structured flow to register, classify and monitor each supplier incident until resolution.

That flow should centralize the affected invoice, the cause of the block, the assigned owner and the current status.

What value it would bring

  • Reduce manual review and follow-up time.
  • Improve financial forecasting by lowering retained invoices.
  • Make owners and resolution times visible.
  • Turn recurring cases into a basis for future improvement and automation.

Conclusion

The proposal turns a scattered and manual task into a traceable, structured and measurable process. The first value is to take the incident out of the personal folder. The second is to prepare a later automation and AI layer on top of that traceability.
