From project to evidence: how to build a living repository of AI-enabled success cases

This project starts from a very specific strategic idea: turning the knowledge generated in Isaval’s projects into a tangible, useful and reusable asset for the organization. The aim is not only to store what we already know more effectively, but to structure it so it can later be activated in marketing, sales and technical positioning.

The starting point was easy to recognize. Information about projects, applied solutions, achieved results and accumulated lessons existed, but it was fragmented across teams, emails, presentations and individual knowledge. That made it difficult to reuse cases, sustain a solid narrative of experience and turn the company’s real track record into a visible advantage in the market.

The problem was not a lack of experience, but a lack of systematization. There was dependence on specific individuals, low commercial reuse of cases, too little real content available for marketing and a structural difficulty in clearly demonstrating accumulated experience. In other words: there was value, but it was not prepared to circulate or scale.

The proposed solution rests on three pillars: simplicity, automation and reuse. The idea is that any team member should be able to register a case in just a few minutes, in a simple format and from an environment accessible even on mobile. From there, artificial intelligence helps enrich, structure and activate that material so each project can become several business assets.

The operating model is organized in three layers. The first is capture: a simple form that makes it easy to register the essence of the case. The second is processing: data structuring and AI-assisted generation to turn dispersed information into useful content. The third is activation: later use of the case in marketing, sales and internal knowledge.

What matters in this approach is that it turns projects into content and content into business value. A well-structured case stops being just a one-off reference and becomes a commercial argument, technical evidence, a positioning asset and internal learning material. The same project can feed a sales sheet, a web story, support during visits or a resource for onboarding and knowledge transfer.

That value extends across several areas. In marketing, it enables continuous content generation based on real experience. In sales, it supports arguments and concrete examples that reinforce credibility and commercial discourse. Internally, it improves training, onboarding and the flow of knowledge between areas.

Several important lessons emerged during the process. First, adoption depends on simplicity: if capturing a case is complicated, the system will not scale. Second, mobile access is key because it brings capture closer to the real moment of the project. Third, AI multiplies the value of the data, but does not remove the need for structure. And fourth, each project can generate multiple assets if it is designed from the start with reuse in mind.

Structure matters more than the tool.

The tool can evolve, but what really matters is defining what information is captured, how it is classified and what later path it will follow. Without that base, no system can turn dispersed experience into a company asset.

Knowledge must be able to circulate.

When a case is trapped inside a presentation or in one person’s memory, its value is limited. When it becomes part of a living, accessible and reusable base, it turns into organizational capability.

Each project can produce more value than it appears to.

Project experience does not only solve a specific job. It can also strengthen the commercial proposition, support technical marketing and demonstrate maturity to clients and the market.

Looking ahead, the system is designed to evolve into a more integrated tool, connected to CRM, the corporate website and commercial environments, while also adding intelligent search and content automation. But even at its current stage, it already points to an important shift: managing knowledge in a more structured way and turning it into competitive advantage.

Ultimately, this project is not only about organizing existing information better. It is about helping Isaval’s accumulated experience stop behaving like dispersed memory and start behaving like a useful, scalable and activatable asset across several business fronts.
