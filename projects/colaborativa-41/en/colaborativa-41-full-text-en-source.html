Collaborative 41. From manual forecast handling to intelligent, scalable planning

Author: Cristian Rodríguez Ferreira

1. Current setup

Planning for strategic customers, initially Leroy Merlin, is currently managed through manual Excel sheets. Spain and Portugal send forecast separately, forcing manual consolidation together with stock, launched production and future requirements.

This model consumes time, depends on individual know-how and slows the company's reaction to forecast changes and future growth.

2. Key problems

Frequent forecast changes, manual version comparison, Spain-Portugal consolidation, risk of overstock or stock-outs, capital tied up by unnecessary purchases, limited traceability and poor scalability to more customers.

3. Objective

Build a scalable internal tool that automates production and purchasing planning, centralizing critical information and improving efficiency, control and growth capacity.

4. Proposed functionalities

Automatic forecast import, multi-file upload by country or customer, automatic demand consolidation, new-versus-old forecast comparison, free-stock reading from ERP, stock and launched-production visibility, automatic future-needs calculation, production recommendation, minimum-lot and full-pallet rounding, risk alerts and historical traceability.

5. Expected benefits

Administrative and operating time savings, fewer errors, lower immobilized inventory, better customer service, stronger reaction capability, more reliable consolidated demand, better coordination across areas and scalability to multiple customers and markets.

6. Recommended phases

Phase 1: MVP with Excel upload and automatic consolidation. Phase 2: ERP integration. Phase 3: advanced analytics and predictive AI. Phase 4: multi-customer rollout and expansion to new areas.

7. Conclusion

The company can turn a manual process that is already internally understood into a scalable strategic tool capable of professionalizing planning, anticipating purchases and supporting future growth.
