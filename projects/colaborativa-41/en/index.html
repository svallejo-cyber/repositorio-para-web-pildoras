Production planning · Isaval

Collaborative 41 · From manual forecast handling to intelligent, scalable planning

Author: Cristian Rodríguez Ferreira
Cristian Rodríguez Ferreira proposes turning manual forecast management for strategic customers into an internal tool that consolidates demand, reads stock and production data, and supports planning with lower inventory and faster reaction capacity.

The current setup

Planning for strategic customers such as Leroy Merlin is still managed through manual Excel files that consolidate Spanish and Portuguese forecast, free stock, launched production and future requirements through significant operational effort and constant review.

The planning logic already exists inside Isaval. The opportunity is to turn it into a system instead of continuing to execute it by hand.

What the current model breaks

Change

Frequent forecast updates force repeated version comparisons and manual reconsolidation.

Inventory

There is a risk of overstock, stock-outs and unnecessary purchasing that ties up capital.

Traceability

Decisions leave too little structured history and rely too heavily on individual knowledge.

Scale

The current model is difficult to replicate for other strategic customers or future markets.

The proposal

Build a scalable internal tool that automates forecast import and consolidation, compares versions, reads stock and production data from the ERP, and calculates future requirements with production recommendations and alerting.

The project is designed in phases: a first MVP with Excel upload and automatic consolidation, then ERP integration and later advanced analytics and predictive AI.

What the business would gain

Lower administrative load, lower immobilized inventory, faster reaction capacity and stronger coordination between planning, purchasing and production. The system would also be ready to scale to more customers and processes without rebuilding the logic every time.

Executive conclusion: this pill is about professionalizing forecast and production planning so it stops being a repeated manual effort and becomes a strategic, scalable and reusable capability.
