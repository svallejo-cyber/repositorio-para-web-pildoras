Practical AI for non-technical business

Executive pill by Santiago Vallejo · Practical self-learning case with Codex · 03/02/2026

Key message

You do not need to be a programmer to drive real AI improvements. You need to detect a real problem, explain it clearly, iterate, and measure.

"I am 57, not a programmer, and still built a functional app that saves me time and errors."

Initial problem

Monthly utility settlement with a tenant was manual, fragmented and error-prone: downloading invoices from 4 providers, transferring to Excel, sending via WhatsApp and checking repayment in banking records.

  • High risk of duplicates, omissions and incorrect manual cross-checks.
  • Strong dependency on memory and available time.
  • Low traceability to validate repayments.
  • Persistent mental load from pending tasks.

What TenantFlow does

Smart intake

Monitors INBOX folder and detects new invoice/receipt PDFs.

Error control

Prevents duplicates with SHA-256 hash per file.

Auto extraction

Reads provider, date, period, amount, reference and utility type.

Operations

Allows corrections, reconciliation and repayment/bank tracking.

Outputs

Generates selected PDF report, annual report and pending receipts pack.

Cycle closure

Sends email from macOS Mail with attachments and BCC traceability.

Operational impact

BeforeNow
Manual, scattered document controlCentralized and traceable process
Frequent duplicates and omissionsDeduplication and automatic extraction
Long, postponed processSimplified flow in minutes
Manual WhatsApp preparationReport + pack + email in one single flow

Build timeline

  • MVP: INBOX scanning + DB + deduplication + base extraction.
  • Interaction: from technical tool to visual app with buttons.
  • Data quality: parser tuning by provider to improve accuracy.
  • Data governance: new operational and reconciliation fields.
  • Usability and reports: practical table + professional outputs.
  • Final automation: contextual email sending with attachments.

What non-technical executives should take away

  • AI is a real productivity lever in admin, commercial and support areas.
  • You can lead without coding if process and success criteria are clear.
  • Focused pilots reduce fear and quickly create internal evidence.
  • Best adoption model: business proposes, IT industrializes.
Executive conclusion: AI productivity is not a theoretical promise; it is a practical discipline that can start now with real problems, iteration and collaboration with IT.
