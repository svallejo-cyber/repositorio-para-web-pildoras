Collaborative 40 · From the manual spreadsheet to intelligent order-status management

Author: Cristian Rodríguez Ferreira
Cristian Rodríguez Ferreira proposes automating the export-order tracking sheet so customer PDFs can be read, checked against stock and production data, and turned into a standardized follow-up file with far less manual effort.

The current problem

Export order-status management still depends on manually reading customer PDFs, reviewing each line, checking stock, manufacturing, batch and production dates, and then transcribing the result into Excel. It is a critical, repetitive workflow heavily tied to individual know-how.

The goal is not only to save time. It is to turn a handcrafted spreadsheet into a stable, scalable and traceable operational capability.

What hurts today

Time

Too many hours are spent on low-value administrative work and manual order-by-order review.

Error

Constant human intervention raises the risk of mistakes in availability, batches, dates and assignment logic.

Scale

When order volume grows, the current process loses speed and robustness.

Dependency

The workflow is not standardized enough and still relies on individual experience that is hard to replicate.

The proposed solution

The proposal is to build a tool able to automatically interpret the customer PDF, cross it with internal databases for stock, production, batches, reservations and dates, and generate the final structured tracking file.

The system would apply the current operating logic consistently, including availability checks, FIFO allocation, detection of work in progress and recommendation of new manufacturing when no stock is available.

What the company gains

The expected improvement is threefold: less operating time, fewer administrative errors and much more homogeneous execution. The output would also be standardized for all customers and become a base for further analysis and future predictive layers.

Executive conclusion: this pill is not only about automating a repetitive task. It is about giving planning a more reliable base to decide, scale and absorb higher volumes without multiplying manual effort.

How it could scale

Cristian proposes starting with export orders, but the same logic could later be extended to domestic orders or similar workflows in NoPaint. That makes it a reusable operational foundation, not a one-off fix.
