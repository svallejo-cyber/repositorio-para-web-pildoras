Collaborative 40. From the manual spreadsheet to intelligent order-status management

Author: Cristian Rodríguez Ferreira

1. Context

The company operates in a highly variable export-order environment where information arrives mainly as PDF documents and still requires manual interpretation.

This workflow, known internally as “the spreadsheet,” has become a critical production-planning activity with a heavy operational burden and strong dependence on individual know-how.

2. Objective

Build an AI-based tool able to automate the reading, interpretation and management of export order statuses.

The goal is to turn a manual process into an automated flow, improving efficiency, reducing errors and creating a stronger base for operational decision-making.

3. Current situation

Orders are received as PDF files by email. Each line is reviewed manually. Stock availability is checked. Manufacturing orders are created when there is a shortage. Batch and production date are validated. FIFO logic is applied manually. Additional information is added for some customers. Final data is transcribed to Excel.

Main limitations: high operating time, elevated human error risk, lack of standardization and poor scalability.

4. Proposed solution

Create a tool that combines intelligent document reading with direct integration to internal databases in order to automate the full workflow, from order interpretation to structured output generation.

5. How it would work

Inputs: customer order PDF and internal databases containing stock, production, batches, reservations and dates.

Automatic process: PDF reading and interpretation, line-by-line analysis and automatic validation of availability.

Decision logic: if stock exists, automatic FIFO batch allocation and manufacturing date inclusion. If stock is not available, identification of work in progress and recommendation of new production.

Output: automatic Excel generation with complete and standardized information per line, including batch and date for every customer.

6. Expected benefits

Operational efficiency, significant reduction in time spent, removal of repetitive low-value tasks, lower human error, consistent FIFO application, process standardization across customers and the ability to absorb higher volume without adding resources.

7. Constraints and future evolution

The solution depends on PDF quality and requires proper integration with internal systems. Later it could incorporate predictive analytics to recommend where to manufacture, optimize quantities and define optimal formats.

8. Conclusion

Automating order-status management would not only reduce time and administrative errors. It would turn a manual workflow dependent on individual knowledge into a scalable, standardized and traceable capability.
