From scattered order to integrated flow

José Carlos Garrigós · Tenth collaborative contribution to the hub on order automation, ERP integration and connection between export, administration and logistics

The problem

In export, orders still arrive through multiple channels and formats: free text in email, PDF, Excel, or even customer-generated documents already using ERP codes. They then have to be reinterpreted, keyed into the system, and followed by invoice and packing list preparation in separate circuits.

The problem is not lack of information. The problem is that the information is spread across email, attachments, ERP, Excel files and logistics, forcing the same data to be reintroduced several times.

What the case proves

The proposal goes beyond reading orders: it outlines a continuous flow able to capture heterogeneous inputs, structure the data the ERP needs, validate it, and then activate the full documentation chain.

This would connect order creation, delivery note, invoice and packing list in one data path, with less rework, less manual dependence and more traceability.

What it adds to the business

Efficiency

It reduces reprocessing and removes part of the repeated manual keying between export, administration and logistics.

Control

It makes it possible to validate customers, references, quantities, units and exceptions before creating the ERP order.

Continuity

It allows the same source data to feed order, invoice and packing list without successive reconstructions.

Memory

It leaves a trace of entry channel, original document, validations and generated documents.

Main lessons

  • Not all customers should be treated equally: the right approach is to start with the most structured ones and expand in waves.
  • Structure matters more than the tool: before automating, it is necessary to define minimum fields, equivalence tables and customer rules.
  • Automation does not remove control; it shifts effort toward exception handling, where human judgment actually adds value.
  • As long as the packing list depends on a parallel logistics sheet, the process will remain broken; the real improvement requires integrating logistics data as well.
Collaborative conclusion: the goal is not only to read orders faster, but to turn a scattered manual intake into a single business flow integrated from incoming email to final shipment documentation.
