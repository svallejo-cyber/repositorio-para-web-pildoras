From daily transcription to a live staffing calendar
Connected management of requests, store coverage and overtime pools to reduce errors and coordinate service better

Raquel Esparza · Thirty-first collaborative contribution to the hub on store staffing, Sebastian requests and day-to-day personnel coordination

The problem

Daily store staffing coordination requires consolidating scattered information from Sebastian, SPCO and multiple spreadsheets to know where each person should be, who covers absences, how Saturdays are distributed and how overtime pools are consumed.

The current routine works, but it consumes time every day and forces manual transcription of data that already exists in different systems, with risk of errors and duplication.

The value of the calendar is not only to organize. It is to make a very sensitive operating process more visible, traceable and fair for both service and people.

What the current model already brings

Raquel already works with an annual calendar that helps visualize store coverage, assign Saturdays more evenly and give staff better personal planning visibility.

It also makes it easier to understand the reason for absences and to follow overtime consumption, which today remains hidden or fragmented across tools.

What the next phase proposes

The proposal is to connect Sebastian requests automatically with the operational calendar, adding rules to ensure minimum staffing per store, role-based allocation and a zone-manager validation filter before any change is applied.

That would allow the solution to remain in Excel or evolve into a more visual panel without losing the core value: a live, connected planning layer with no manual transcription.

What would improve

Less duplication

Avoid omissions and mistakes by stopping the same information from being copied and reviewed in multiple places.

More traceability

Link requests, absences and overtime pools without checking separate tools.

More autonomy

Enable more direct consultation for store personnel without always depending on the zone manager.

Better coverage

Monitor more safely that every store meets its minimum staffing requirements.

Why it fits the Hub

The piece does not need a fully built AI solution behind it to be valuable. What matters is that it identifies a real organizational friction and a clear path to automate a significant part of the coordination work.

Tools such as Codex or a lightweight programming layer can make that connection feasible without starting from a heavy custom development.
