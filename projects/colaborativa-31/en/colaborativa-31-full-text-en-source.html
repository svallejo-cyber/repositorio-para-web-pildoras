From daily transcription to a live staffing calendar: Connected management of requests, store coverage and overtime pools to reduce errors and coordinate service better

Context

Store staffing coordination currently relies on an annual Excel calendar plus supporting sources such as Sebastian and SPCO.

That calendar already provides structure and visibility, but it still requires manual consolidation of absences, support routes, coverage, Saturdays and overtime pools.

Problem identified

There is an almost daily routine of transcription and review that consumes time and may introduce errors.

Critical information such as the reason for an absence or the real overtime consumption remains split across systems and is not always easy to consult in one place.

What is proposed

Automatically connect Sebastian requests with the operational calendar, adding store-level rules, role control, zone-manager validation and automatic update afterwards.

The solution could remain in Excel or evolve into a more visual panel.

Benefits

  • Avoid duplication and omissions.
  • Improve traceability of overtime generation and consumption.
  • Enable more autonomous consultation for staff.
  • Monitor minimum staffing coverage per store more reliably.

Conclusion

The proposal does not start from a large finished technology solution, but from a real coordination need. That is exactly why it fits well in the Hub: it identifies a clear operational friction and a viable path to automate it and make it safer.
