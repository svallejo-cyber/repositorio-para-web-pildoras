Collaborative 43. Transforming fitness-certificate review: from mass checking to automated inconsistency detection

Author: María José Mancebo

1. Starting point

Fitness-certificate review in occupational health requires validating fitness status, identity, role, center and coherence between applied medical protocols and the risks defined for each function.

2. Current problem

Certificates are still reviewed one by one in a high-volume environment with many roles and work centers, even though not every certificate needs the same level of technical attention.

3. Risks in the current model

High technical time consumption, higher human-error risk in repetitive tasks, poor scalability and too much focus on routine verification instead of preventive judgment.

4. Proposal

Automate base validation of data, role, center and applied protocols, detect inconsistencies and reserve human review for incidents, deviations or complex cases.

5. What it would bring

Greater efficiency, stronger reliability, less error, more consistency and better alignment between applied protocols and actual job risks.

6. Key learning

The value does not lie in reviewing everything in the same way, but in automatically locating inconsistency so technical attention can focus where it matters.
