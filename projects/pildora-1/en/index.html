Pill 2 · Something big is happening

Starting point of the project: why AI already requires a strategic reading, not only a technical one

Starting point

This is not a passing trend or a temporary management conversation. It is a change with real capacity to alter how work is analyzed, coordinated, decided, and executed inside the company.

The core conviction is simple: we should not observe this opportunity from the sidelines. We should understand it early, test it with method, and prepare ourselves to lead it wherever it can create real impact.

The signal coming from the market

The discussion around Matt Shumer's post points to a meaningful acceleration in AI capabilities. This is no longer only about assistants that help. It is about systems and agents able to execute complex tasks with greater autonomy and practical usefulness.

If that reading is correct, the advantage will not belong to those who talk about AI first, but to those who start experimenting with it first on real processes.

The counterpoint worth keeping

Ignacio de la Torre's article introduces an important correction: today there is still not enough evidence to say that we are already facing massive and immediate job destruction caused directly by AI.

That nuance does not weaken the strategic argument. It improves it. It forces a more rigorous reading of the moment, avoiding both naive enthusiasm and simplistic alarmism.

Our executive reading

Real change

The first visible impact may not be mass unemployment, but a widening gap between those who learn early and those who do not move.

Accumulating advantage

Those who identify useful processes, experiment, and learn early will build an advantage that is hard to recover later.

Business first

The central issue is not technology itself. It is detecting where there is friction, cost, slowness, or disorder and redesigning it with business judgment.

Method

This requires concrete sessions, real cases, discipline, and shared learning. Not abstract conversations.

Executive conclusion: the prudent move today is not to wait. The prudent move is to begin understanding, experimenting, and learning before the next jump finds us reacting too late.
