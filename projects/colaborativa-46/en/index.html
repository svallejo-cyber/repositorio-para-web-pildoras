Industrial maintenance · Isaval

Collaborative 46 · From manual tickets to structured, predictive maintenance

Author: Juan Manuel Marín Caparros
Juan Manuel proposes moving maintenance from a reactive, document-heavy model toward a more structured, traceable system ready for automatic classification, pattern detection and failure prediction.

Current setup

Maintenance still depends heavily on manual descriptions, subjective classification, informal communication and implicit human knowledge. The problem is no longer only technical: it is structural, documentary and operational.

AI will only create real value if the base is first put in order: typologies, criticality, root cause and minimum data quality.

What distorts the current system

Mixing

The same ticket type groups real breakdowns, cleaning tasks, requests, inspections and routine work, distorting KPIs.

Description

Many tickets do not reflect root cause, impact or criticality, forcing inconsistent manual interpretation.

Communication

There are late warnings, open incidents without direct contact and artificially inflated response times due to weak coordination.

Prediction

The system records incidents, but does not learn from them or detect patterns, recurrence or future risk.

Proposed evolution

The proposal starts by structuring maintenance properly and then applying AI to reclassify tickets, detect recurring incidents, prioritize better and prepare real predictive maintenance.

AI should act as a system for classification, monitoring, analysis, prediction and decision support for technicians and maintenance leaders.

What AI could add

Classification

Automatic reclassification of tickets to distinguish real corrective work, preventive tasks, cleaning, calibration, misuse and improvement requests.

Patterns

Detection of machines, components or shifts with abnormal repetition of failures and recurrence that today remain invisible.

Assistance

Support for technicians with likely causes, common spare parts, similar past cases and real average repair times.

Escalation

Intelligent incident communication to distinguish real urgency and trigger immediate alerts when criticality requires it.

What Isaval gains

More reliable KPIs, fewer repeated failures, better planning, fewer unplanned stoppages, longer equipment life and a much stronger documentary base for audit, traceability and decision-making.

Executive conclusion: this pill proposes using AI not as cosmetic technology on top of maintenance tickets, but as a lever to reorganize maintenance and turn it into a more objective, predictive and governable capability.
