Digitalisation and operational optimisation in the packaging section

Starting point

The packaging section is currently organised into two main areas, water-based and solvent-based. In each one, a controller works alongside three operators per shift, responsible for carrying out the activities associated with packaging the different batches.

The current operating model relies largely on direct coordination and verbal communication between team members. Although that system is supported by the team’s experience and knowledge, it also has clear limitations: high dependence on individual judgement, difficulty in setting objective priorities and low traceability in day-to-day operational management.

Available base

The organisation already has an operational database coming from the ERP and a productivity dashboard that allows indicators such as availability, performance and productivity to be analysed. This provides a solid base for evolving towards a more digital, data-driven operating model.

Solution concept

The proposal is a digital tool acting as an intelligent operational-planning system, able to integrate production data and automatically generate an optimal work sequence for each shift and each packaging area.

The tool would rely on information that is already available, such as production, timings and performance, and would enable the operation to move from simple visualisation towards a more active form of management based on real operational behaviour.

Main functions

  • Organisation and planning of packaging batches according to real production needs.
  • Automatic prioritisation based on stock-break risk, confirmed orders and production planning.
  • Dynamic task allocation across the available operators.
  • Real-time adaptation to incidents, priority changes or deviations.
  • Inclusion of line cleaning, format changes, product changes and preparation tasks.
  • Unified operational visualisation aligned with the dashboard already developed.

The role of operational intelligence

The system would incorporate progressive learning from historical and operational data such as actual packaging times, speeds by format or product, order frequency, the impact of product changes and recurring incidents.

Over time, that logic would make it possible to adjust planning automatically, reduce inefficiencies and improve the quality of everyday decision-making on the shopfloor.

Expected benefits

  • Greater organisation and standardisation of packaging work.
  • Less dependence on individual knowledge.
  • Objective prioritisation based on real data.
  • Better stock management and fewer stock-outs.
  • Higher operational efficiency and improved metrics such as productivity or OEE.
  • Greater traceability in decision-making and the possibility of extending the model to other plant sections.

Evolution vision

The underlying ambition is to move towards a more digital, connected and data-based model for managing packaging and production, capable of continuously optimising everyday operations and turning plant information into shared operational judgement.
