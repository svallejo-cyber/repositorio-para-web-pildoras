From opaque returns to traceable blocked stock handling

Celia López González · Twenty-second collaborative contribution to the hub on returns handling, blocked stock and end-to-end traceability from intake to final destination

The problem

Handling returned goods and blocked stock in the warehouse currently makes fast and reliable traceability difficult. Part of the issue is that returns are not followed end to end, and blocked material already in storage is not organised under a common operating logic.

The improvement is not only about checking blocked material better. It is about tracing the entire flow from the moment a return starts until the material is reincorporated, recycled, donated or disposed of.

What this initiative proposes

Celia proposes a database-connected tool that allows warehouse, commercial and planning teams to share information without relying on scattered spreadsheets. The system should cover both new returns and material already blocked in storage.

For the latter, existing stock has already started to be organised through destination lots: 20001 for waste, 20002 for recycling and 20004 for donation. In addition, virtual reconditioning locations are proposed for certain damaged containers so they do not enter finished-goods storage as blocked stock awaiting treatment.

What it would bring to the business

Traceability

Clear monitoring of material from physical receipt of the return to its final destination.

Capacity

Less blocked stock and less saturation of return-related locations in the warehouse.

Efficiency

Less time spent on returns, reprocessing and recycling, with lower dependence on disconnected spreadsheets.

Sustainability

More reuse, more recycling and less material ending as waste because of poor visibility or delayed action.

Where AI fits

The proposal goes beyond organising data. AI could automatically analyse histories of customer, product, lot, season, carrier, incident type or return timing to detect patterns that are not manually visible.

  • Products or lots with higher return rates.
  • Stores or customers with higher incident ratios.
  • Carriers with more breakages.
  • Materials repeatedly ending in waste or obsolescence.
  • Recurring errors in preparation, planning or transport.
Collaborative conclusion: Celia López’s proposal turns returns and blocked stock into a traceable and shared workflow, supported by an operational data base that also prepares the ground for moving from reactive handling to predictive management.
