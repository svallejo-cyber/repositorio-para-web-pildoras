Returns handling and blocked stock

Purpose

The proposal aims to facilitate the tracking and final destination of returned or blocked material in the finished-goods warehouse. The current problem is that returns management and the location of blocked material make quick and reliable traceability difficult, as well as reuse or disposal decisions.

Change of approach

A change is proposed from the very beginning of the return until its final destination. The process starts with the physical receipt of returned material, document verification, classification under defined parameters, assignment to a destination location with a new identifying lot and subsequent warehouse placement.

The process ends when the product leaves the returns flow towards its final destination: reincorporation into finished-goods stock, effective recycling, donation/export or disposal as waste.

Shared database and destination lots

To share information with warehouse, commercial and planning teams without depending on spreadsheets that get lost or outdated, the proposal is to work with database-connected applications that make blocked material visible and easier to manage quickly.

As part of this logic, three destination lots have already been created for material already stored in the warehouse: 20001 waste destination, 20002 recycling destination and 20004 donation destination.

Virtual reconditioning locations have also been requested for water-based and solvent-based products when containers return stained or damaged, preventing them from entering finished-goods storage as blocked lots awaiting treatment.

What AI can add

AI could automatically analyse large volumes of historical data taking into account customer, product, lot, season, logistics channel, incident type, return timing and storage or transport conditions.

  • Products with higher return rates.
  • Customers or stores with higher incident ratios.
  • Problematic lots or recurrent incident lots.
  • Campaigns or seasons with higher return volumes.
  • Carriers with more breakages.
  • Materials that repeatedly end in obsolescence, waste or unexecuted donation.
  • Repeated mistakes in preparation or planning.

Expected impact

On warehouse operations: less blocked stock from the origin, more available capacity and less saturation in return locations. On manufacturing: fewer interruptions due to reprocessing or recycling, less time spent on returns and optimisation of costs tied to packaging, recycling and disposal. On sustainability: less waste, more reuse and recycling, and real activation of donations.

Conclusion

AI would make it possible to move from a reactive process, where returns are managed only once they arrive, to a predictive process where risks are anticipated, patterns are identified and blocked stock is reduced from the source.
