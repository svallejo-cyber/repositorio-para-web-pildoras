From email threads to a traceable validation flow

Context

Packaging design validation is still handled through email. PDFs are heavy, versions overlap, comments get scattered and there is no formal record of who approved what, when they did it and on which exact version.

The process works because someone holds it together, but traceability depends on memory, manual follow-up and the ability to reconstruct Outlook threads when production or labeling need to know which design was finally approved.

The reference model

The collaboration with ADEO showed a better way of working: a platform where each reference has its own space, comments are anchored to the design, approvals are recorded and everyone knows the exact status of the validation.

That learning has not remained as an external observation. It has become the basis for defining an internal flow and building a first functional pilot adapted to Isaval’s reality.

What has been built

With AI support, a local functional pilot of a packaging-validation web platform has been built. The manager creates a reference by searching the product through its EAN in the catalog, uploads the design PDF and assigns reviewers with a deadline.

Reviewers receive the task in a pending panel, open the PDF directly in the browser and leave comments anchored to the exact point on the design. Each reviewer approves or rejects independently.

Expected workflow

  • Reference creation and PDF upload.
  • Reviewer assignment and deadline definition.
  • Anchored comments on the design, without descriptive email exchanges.
  • New version upload when a rejection occurs.
  • Comparison between previous and corrected versions.
  • Automatic move to Internally validated once all reviewers approve.
  • Dashboard with traffic-light status and filters by product, status or reviewer.
  • Consultation access for other teams to the final design and approval history.

What it contributes

The value is not only in organizing the current flow. It lies in transforming a fragile email-based process into an internal tool with traceability, visibility and the ability to scale without losing control.

And even if final deployment in the company environment still requires joint work with Systems, the pilot has already solved something decisive: turning a diffuse need into a very precise operating specification.

What it opens next

Beyond the packaging case, this work points to an important underlying line: AI makes it possible to build tailored internal tools for processes that previously could only be handled by third-party services or manual email-driven workflows.

The same approach could extend to other validation or review flows that follow the same pattern.

Conclusion

The proposal turns email exchange into a traceable, visible and scalable process. And it shows that AI does not only help analyze or write: it can also open the door to building internal tools aligned exactly with how the company works.
