From the annual consultancy to in-house carbon footprint calculation

Sergio Huertas · Thirty-third collaborative contribution to the hub on emissions calculation automation, recurring savings and in-house generation of the technical report

The problem

Calculating the company’s annual carbon footprint is both a legal requirement and an increasingly relevant input for sustainability reporting and customer conversations. Until now, this work had been outsourced for around 5,000-6,000 euros per year, with long lead times and a significant manual data-gathering burden for Beatriz.

The opportunity here was not to build a flashy AI showcase. It was to internalize a mandatory calculation, reduce administrative time and prepare the company to monitor emissions much more continuously.

What has been built

Sergio developed an application that calculates the company’s carbon footprint for Scopes 1, 2 and 3, using official MITECO rules for S1 and S2 and historically validated criteria from previous external reports for S3.

The tool allows the user to enter consumption data, calculate CO2 emissions, consolidate results by scope and generate an internal technical report similar to the one previously produced by the consultancy.

Why it has real value

Savings

It reduces the recurring annual cost of an external consultancy for a process that is now structurally required.

Time

It shortens the administrative cycle needed to gather data and wait for the final report.

Accuracy

Tests show differences of no more than 0.001% versus calculations already delivered by external consultants.

Scalability

The next step is clear: connect it to ERP and Docuware so the calculation no longer depends on manual input.

What the logbook confirms

  • The methodological backend already covers validation and calculation logic for Scopes 1, 2 and 3.
  • The application already generates a corporate PDF report with methodology, results, traceability and appendices.
  • MITECO rules, dual Scope 2 reporting and numerical validation against real reports have already been tested.
  • The next technical step is already identified: connect internal systems so the process stops depending on manual loading.

Deeper reading

This piece is valuable precisely because it does not stop at AI rhetoric. It solves a concrete compliance and sustainability need, reduces external dependency and converts methodological know-how into a reusable internal tool.

It also opens a more ambitious path: moving from an outsourced annual report to an internal capability for continuous emissions tracking.

Collaborative conclusion: Sergio Huertas’s proposal turns a mandatory outsourced calculation into a precise, traceable and scalable internal capability, with recurring economic savings and a real basis to automate environmental monitoring inside Isaval.
