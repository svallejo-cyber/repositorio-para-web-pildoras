Need for controlled access to real product costs for commercial decisions

Context

Within the commercial area, especially in export and international operations, there are deals where the usual logic of general tariff plus discount is not enough to decide accurately. Heterogeneous markets, special projects, one-off high-volume deals, subsidiaries or tenders sometimes require direct gross-margin analysis before making a decision.

Current problem

Today, the real cost data is not directly available to the commercial area. When needed, it must be requested from the technical department or laboratory, which holds the updated databases and provides the current product cost.

This system protects sensitive information, but it also creates operational dependency, slows commercial reaction time and forces the team to rely on third parties for a data point that, at certain decision levels, may be needed quickly.

Identified need

The proposal is to study a tool or system that allows the consultation of the updated cost of a specific product, in a specific format or size and at a precise moment, with access reserved only for authorised profiles.

This is not an open or generalised access model, but a highly restricted, controlled and traceable access limited to certain responsibility levels such as commercial management or, at most, selected regional leaders with real decision capacity.

Expected design

The tool should be designed with a high level of confidentiality, permission control and usage traceability so that the data can be used when genuinely needed without compromising information security.

Expected value

A solution of this kind would reduce continuous dependency on the technical department for point cost queries, speed up the preparation and negotiation of special operations and enable faster, better-grounded decisions in export and international operations.

In contexts such as subsidiaries, tenders or special projects, this visibility would help refine economic proposals, identify available margin more precisely and improve competitiveness in specific operations without losing sight of required profitability.

Conclusion

The need is to have a limited, secure and updated access system to real product costs for selected decision-making profiles, so they can analyse gross margins in specific operations with greater precision, speed and autonomy, without depending each time on a manual query to the technical department or laboratory.
