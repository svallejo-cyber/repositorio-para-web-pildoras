From standard discounting to controlled access to real cost

José Carlos Garrigós · Twentieth collaborative contribution to the hub on restricted access to real product costs for special commercial decisions in export operations

The problem

In export and international operations, working only with tariff and discount is not always enough. There are special operations, tenders, subsidiaries or concentrated-volume deals where the commercial decision requires a more precise view of gross margin before closing an offer.

The proposal is not to open real cost information to the whole organisation. It is to allow very restricted, traceable and secure access for people who need to decide quickly how far a negotiation can go without losing profitability control.

What this initiative proposes

The proposal defines a tool or system that would allow the consultation of the updated real cost of a specific product, in a specific format and at a precise moment, only for authorised profiles such as commercial management or certain decision-capable regional leaders.

The goal is to reduce operational dependency on the technical department or laboratory for point cost queries while keeping a high level of confidentiality, permission control and access traceability.

What it would bring to the business

Speed

Faster response in tenders, special projects and negotiations where margin must be evaluated urgently.

Control

Restricted and traceable access to sensitive information without making real cost openly available.

Autonomy

Less dependence on manual queries to technical teams or the lab for high-level commercial decisions.

Profitability

Better ability to tune proposals, improve competitiveness and protect the minimum required margin in each operation.

Key learnings

  • There are international commercial decisions where tariff and discount do not offer enough precision.
  • Access to real cost should not be open, but it also should not be fully inaccessible when the decision requires speed.
  • The key is to design a safe, reserved and auditable access model, not to democratise a sensitive data point.
  • A tool of this kind improves commercial speed without breaking governance over critical information.
Collaborative conclusion: José Carlos Garrigós’ proposal defines controlled access to real product costs for selected decision-making profiles, with the aim of improving commercial precision, negotiation speed and profitability judgement in special operations.
