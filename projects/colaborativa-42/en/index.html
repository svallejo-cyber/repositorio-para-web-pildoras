Stores, branches and maintenance · Isaval

Collaborative 42 · From experience-based openings to a historical decision system for stores and maintenance

Author: Rubén Vicent
Rubén Vicent proposes turning historical information on premises, refurbishments and maintenance into an internal tool that compares openings, anticipates investment, estimates lead times and gives aggregated control over the lifecycle of stores and branches.

The current setup

New store or branch openings and their maintenance still rely on technical criteria, previous experience and dispersed information, mainly in OneDrive, without a shared comparison tool.

The company already owns the historical information. The next step is turning it into reusable operating judgment for both opening decisions and later maintenance.

What the current model breaks

Choice

Site selection still carries too much risk because location, access, size and condition are not compared against an objective shared base.

Cost

Refurbishment estimates and investment ranges are not standardized, which opens the door to deviations and weaker decisions.

Timing

Opening and works planning are slower because time know-how lives inside prior projects instead of a living decision system.

Maintenance

Maintenance incidents and spending are not read globally by zone or typology, limiting prevention and negotiation capacity.

The proposal

Build an internal analysis and decision-support tool that centralizes historical information on premises, budgets, works, images, Power BI and maintenance to compare, estimate and prioritize with stronger judgment.

The scope is not limited to new openings: it also creates a continuous view of maintenance to understand recurrent incidents, spend by branch and opportunities for corrective action.

Where it would apply first

Premises

Objective comparison of sites by location, connectivity, size, physical characteristics and current condition before committing to an opening.

Refurbishment

Cost estimation through similar projects and real €/m² ranges to rule out non-viable sites before signing them.

Lead time and works

Schedule estimation from before/after photographs, visual progress follow-up and objective validation against certifications.

Maintenance

Reading recurrent incidents and spend by intervention or branch to detect deterioration, negotiate better and anticipate budget.

What Isaval would gain

Better opening decisions, lower risk, stronger budget control, more visibility over projects and maintenance, stronger standardization and better operating efficiency across the teams involved.

Executive conclusion: this pill proposes moving from opening and maintaining through dispersed know-how to doing it through a historical system that turns accumulated experience into shared, measurable and reusable judgment.
