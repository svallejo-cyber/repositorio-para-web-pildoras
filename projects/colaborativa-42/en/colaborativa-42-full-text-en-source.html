Collaborative 42. From experience-based openings to a historical decision system for stores and maintenance

Author: Rubén Vicent

1. Current setup

New store or branch openings and their maintenance are still handled through previous experience, dispersed information and limited structured comparison capacity.

Most relevant data still lives in OneDrive, and the company lacks a shared tool to compare sites, estimate refurbishment cost, anticipate lead times or read maintenance globally.

2. Key problems

Risk in site selection, refurbishment cost deviations, inefficient opening planning and no aggregated control of maintenance incidents or spending.

3. Objective

Build an internal analysis and decision-support tool that centralizes historical information on premises, refurbishments, images, budgets, Power BI and maintenance in order to decide better.

4. Use in new openings

Objective comparison of premises by location, connectivity, size, physical characteristics and current condition; cost estimation through similar projects; schedule estimation from before/after photographs and prior works; work progress follow-up and coordination across departments.

5. Use in maintenance

Identification of recurrent incidents, cost estimation by intervention type and comparative reading of maintenance spend by branch in order to detect deterioration, negotiate better and anticipate budget.

6. Expected benefits

Better opening decisions, lower risk, stronger budget control, more visibility over projects and maintenance, greater process standardization and higher operating efficiency.

7. Next steps

Functional definition of the tool and inventory/structuring of existing data from OneDrive and Power BI.
