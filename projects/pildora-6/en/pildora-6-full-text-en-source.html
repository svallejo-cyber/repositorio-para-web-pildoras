Pill 7. AI is not about writing nicely. It is about changing how a company works

Team,

It is worth pausing and putting some order into this, because I still have the impression that we are mixing things up.

For many people, artificial intelligence still means ChatGPT. And ChatGPT does indeed help write better, summarize documents, prepare a reasonable presentation, or generate a striking image. All of that is true. But stopping there means stopping at the surface.

AI was not born now. It has been developing for decades. What has changed is that, finally, three things have come together at the same time: a lot of data, a lot of computing power, and much better models.

And there is one important detail here: without the famous GPUs, without that parallel computing muscle, we would not be talking about this in these terms. Behind the apparent miracle there is a huge amount of infrastructure, energy, and investment.

Then came models capable of learning at scale. First they saw better. Then they understood language better. And now they are starting to do something much more relevant: execute.

That, for me, is the real shift.

ChatGPT is a great entry point. But it is not “AI” itself. It is a very polished interface for interacting with a family of models. And if we reduce AI to writing better, summarizing meetings, or making curious images, we are treating it like a toy just when it is starting to sit at the operations table.

The real evolution has been this:

First the models understood.
Then they responded.
Then they created content.
Now they are starting to do work.

That is the serious difference.

Tools such as Codex or the more advanced Claude environments no longer stay only in conversation. They can work on files, organize information, build a small application, turn a text into a website, review code, or leave something working.

It is not only that they help. It is that they are beginning to execute.

And for a company like ours, that matters much more than any flashy demonstration.

Until recently, between an idea and a solution there was a long path: detect the need, explain it, convince people, bring in IT, wait for a slot, design, test, and correct. That path will continue to exist for many deeper things, as it should. But between idea and execution a new middle path has appeared: much faster, cheaper, and more accessible.

And that changes the very nature of competitive advantage.

Because now one person with business judgment, mental clarity, and some perseverance can move a very long way on their own. Not because they have become a programmer, but because the distance between thinking and prototyping has been radically shortened.

That is the central point.

We are not facing a technology for decorating work. We are facing a technology that can change the speed at which a company detects a problem, analyzes an opportunity, builds a provisional solution, and tests it.

Let me give a very close example.

Imagine we want to understand why one product family is losing margin in a specific area while incidents, promotional pressure, and a certain commercial sense of wear are all increasing.

Until now, that usually ends in emails, calls, spreadsheets, different versions of the data, and partial interpretations.

In the new scenario, a well-built agent could receive a simple instruction: cross sales, margin, mix, discounts, returns, incidents, claims, and customer behavior in a specific area, detect anomalies, propose reasonable hypotheses, and return an executive summary with lines of action.

That no longer sounds like science fiction. It sounds like something that is starting to come within reach.

That agent could go to the ERP, the CRM, the commercial history, and internal systems, cross everything in minutes, and return a very valuable first layer of work.

That is the difference between using AI to “write better” and using AI to change how a company works.

And here I want to introduce a more personal idea.

I am, honestly, scared.

Not in an apocalyptic sense. I am not talking about childish fear of the machine. I am talking about the fairly firm intuition that something very big is going to happen in a very short time, and that this change is not going to distribute its effects in a gentle or homogeneous way.

I believe there will be few winners and many losers.

Winners: companies that understand early what is happening, take care of their data, mobilize their strong people, and learn to integrate these agents into the way they work.

Losers: those that trivialize the phenomenon, reduce it to “making nice texts,” or trust that there is still plenty of time.

And exactly the same on the professional side.

Those who will come out stronger are the ones with curiosity, openness of mind, discipline to learn, and courage to try. Those who wait for the perfect course, the definitive explanation, or the formal instruction to start will be left behind.

This time the risk is not only in making mistakes. The real risk may be in arriving late.

But the other half of the truth must also be said: all of this rests on a very unsexy but absolutely critical condition: data.

The better these agents become, the more important it is for data to be well captured, well named, well ordered, well maintained, and well governed. A brilliant agent fed with poor or contradictory data does not solve the problem. It accelerates it.

That is why I insist so much that data is not a secondary matter or a “systems” topic. It is a strategic raw material.

And the underlying movement, in my view, is this: artificial intelligence is ceasing to be a support tool and is becoming a new digital layer of work inside the company.

First it saw.
Then it read.
Then it wrote.
Now it is beginning to execute.

And in that transition the center of gravity of value is moving.

If I had to summarize this entire story in one idea, it would be this: AI is no longer only about generating content; it is about multiplying the capacity for well-directed work inside a company.

And anyone who still believes this is mainly about nice texts, curious images, or amusing videos has still not entered the important part of the story.
