From an overgrown catalog to a portfolio with judgment

Iván Morales · Thirty-eighth collaborative contribution to the hub on a tool designed to read the portfolio better, detect overlap, and organize the catalog with technical and economic judgment

The problem

When a catalog grows for years without a sufficiently rigorous review, the company can end up carrying complexity that does not always provide proportional value. As Iván points out, there are likely overlap zones where products compete with each other without enough differentiation in technical performance, price, or channel.

The opportunity is not only to cut references. It is to organize the portfolio so each product has a clearer logic in the market and inside the company itself.

What the piece proposes

The idea is to develop a tool able to cross sales data, technical information, and company-defined criteria in order to return a much more useful reading of the catalog. The goal is to identify overlap, positioning inconsistencies, and rationalization opportunities without putting a relevant part of current revenue at risk.

Diagnosis

Detect products that compete against each other, positioning gaps, and internal cannibalization zones.

Decision

Provide criteria to keep, review, or rationalize references on a more objective basis.

Speed

Make viable an analysis that would otherwise be long, tedious, and hard to sustain manually.

Scalability

Prepare a base that can connect with margin and profitability analyses already under development.

Why AI makes sense here

AI does not replace business judgment here. It accelerates it. It allows large volumes of data to be analyzed under company-defined rules and turned into a much more useful visual reading of problems and opportunities inside the portfolio.

Its value lies in turning a slow and manual exercise into a much more recurrent and governable analytical capability.

Strategic reading

This proposal points to a structural issue: an overly large catalog does not only complicate commercial management. It also makes it harder to read what really sells, where overlap exists, what each family contributes in margin terms, and what deserves to remain because of its true market position.

The logic of the piece is not simplification for its own sake. It is portfolio coherence so technical performance, sales, price, and profitability can be read together.

Collaborative conclusion: Iván Morales proposes turning catalog review into a recurring analytical capability, supported by data and ready to reduce complexity, detect overlap, and support more informed economic and strategic decisions.
