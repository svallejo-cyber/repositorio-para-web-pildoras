Collaborative pill · Isaval team

From strategic question to data-driven answer

Maria Vallejo · Eighth collaborative contribution to the hub on management questions, consolidation of scattered information and navigable competitive analysis tools

The problem

Management teams often raise strategic questions that everyone sees as important, but that cannot always be answered with immediate data: geographic presence, competitive opening hours, growth white spaces or comparative positioning against competitors.

Knowledge exists inside the organization, but it lives in fragments. AI makes it possible to turn that dispersion into a unified, comparable and navigable view.

What the case proves

The proposal shows two concrete examples: an interactive map comparing Isaval stores against more than 450 competitor locations, and an opening-hours comparison that detects meaningful differences with Monto and Valentine.

What would previously have required weeks of consulting work and manual consolidation becomes a dynamic tool that supports strategic conversations in real time.

What it adds to the business

Speed

It drastically reduces the time between the executive question and an answer that can be used with data.

Global view

It turns dispersed market knowledge into a complete comparable picture that is easy to explore.

Replicability

It opens a reusable model for other strategic questions with heterogeneous sources and expensive analysis.

Judgment

It does not replace executive judgment; it strengthens it by preparing the reading and contrast of information better.

Main lesson

  • Many strategic questions remain unanswered because the cost of gathering and comparing data is too high.
  • AI reduces that cost and makes it viable to turn scattered knowledge into actual analysis.
  • The value lies not only in answering one question, but in enabling a new layer of visibility for management.
  • The pattern can be extended to other market, product or competitive-positioning comparisons.
Collaborative conclusion: AI does not replace field knowledge, but it can turn that knowledge into a more global, faster and more useful capability for supporting strategic decisions.
