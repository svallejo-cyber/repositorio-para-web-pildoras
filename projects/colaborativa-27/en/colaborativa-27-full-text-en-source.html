From one-off control to distributed self-control: Alerts on pricing, discounts and below-cost sales

Context

Commercial activity combines tariffs, promotions, special customer conditions, channel-specific rules and product-level exceptions.

The core issue is turning that fragmented logic into a recurring control system that can handle thousands of sales lines.

Identified problem

Finding anomalous price or discount operations used to be slow, difficult to scale and highly dependent on manual review.

For part of the year, outdated cost references in paint also reduced control precision and speed.

What has been built

Using live paint cost data, Paco built a model that flags below-cost sales, excessive prices, excessive discounts and other anomalies under business-specific rules.

Measures calculate the real discount, compare it against allowed limits and label operations automatically.

Role of AI and automatic distribution

AI helped translate complex commercial rules into working DAX measures and accelerate validation cycles.

Power Automate then distributes the findings to salespeople, regional managers and line managers, enabling self-control.

Operational result and conclusion

The process has been running weekly for months and reduces control effort to around one hour per cycle.

Its most relevant effect is behavioral: cases are often corrected before the controller steps in, so control becomes recurring, distributed and actionable.
