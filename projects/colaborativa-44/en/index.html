Purchasing and supply chain · Isaval

Collaborative 44 · From Excel and email to intelligent supplier-order tracking

Author: Carmen Vicente Zaragoza
Carmen Vicente proposes turning manual supplier-order follow-up into a system that reads confirmations, centralizes order status, alerts on delays and anticipates supply risks before they affect production.

The current setup

Supplier-order follow-up still depends on Excel, email review and manual capture of confirmations. Delivery dates, incidents and commitments live across several sources and require constant intervention from the purchasing team.

The organization does not just need better order registration. It needs to anticipate delays before they impact stock, planning and production.

What the current model breaks

Capture

Confirmations and attachments are read manually from emails and PDFs, consuming too much repetitive time.

Visibility

There is no centralized, real-time view of the status of each supplier order.

Risk

Delays are followed reactively, creating stock-out risk and unnecessary replanning.

Traceability

The company lacks structured history of lead times, compliance and supplier performance.

The proposal

The proposed solution is an internal intelligent follow-up system able to read order confirmations, centralize status, monitor dates and trigger proactive alerts when delays or supply uncertainty appear.

This would move the model from reactive and highly individual knowledge-based management to a more controlled, visible and eventually predictive capability.

Core capabilities

Reading

Automatic extraction of relevant information from supplier emails and attached PDFs.

Status

Centralized order tracking: sent, confirmed, dated, delayed or received.

Alerts

Automatic confirmation requests and early warnings when delivery commitments drift.

Analysis

Lead-time calculation by supplier and dashboard support for purchasing and planning decisions.

What Isaval would gain

Less manual work, stronger supply visibility, better coordination between purchasing, planning and production, and more capacity to anticipate manufacturing impact before it becomes an operating issue.

Executive conclusion: this pill proposes turning supplier-order follow-up into a visible and traceable operating capability that reduces supply risk and improves the stability of production planning.
