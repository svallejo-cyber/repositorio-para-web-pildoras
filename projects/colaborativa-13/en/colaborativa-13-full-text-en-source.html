Smart Digitalization of the Technical Assistance Service (SAT)

Starting point

The Technical Assistance Service team currently spends a meaningful share of its time on manual work such as logging consultations and incidents, dealing with fragmented communications, manually classifying requests, handling data in Excel and searching for information without real-time visibility.

This creates operational inefficiencies, slows decision-making and limits the exploitation of the knowledge generated by SAT itself.

What the project proposes

The proposal is to implement a centralized digital platform that structures information from the source and enables, in a second layer, intelligent exploitation through AI.

  • Digital capture of communications from email, calls and WhatsApp.
  • Structured classification of incidents by type, product family, geography and priority.
  • Integrated case management with statuses, attached documentation and full traceability.
  • Real-time operational follow-up and KPI exploitation without manual Excel treatment.
  • Subsequent AI layer for recurrence detection, pattern recognition, alerts, response suggestions and technical reports.

Where AI would add value

Once information is structured, AI can add value through automatic identification of recurrent problems, pattern detection by product, geography or customer, early alerts, prediction of incidents and deviations, customer-response suggestions, solution recommendations based on historical cases and automatic generation of technical reports.

AI does not replace the system. It learns from a previously ordered system and improves the SAT team's agility and consistency.

Expected benefits

  • Significant reduction in operational time.
  • Structured data from the source and gradual elimination of manual tasks.
  • Better decision-making and more time dedicated to customer-value work.
  • Integrated incident control and stronger information traceability.
  • Growing analytical and anticipatory capacity.

Implementation reading

The correct sequence is clear: first close capture, taxonomy, workflow and data; then exploit KPIs and comparisons; and only then activate more advanced learning, alert and recommendation capabilities. The model thus evolves from Manual to Digital and, finally, to Intelligent.

Final idea

The project is not only about digitizing SAT. It is about transforming a service that today records and reacts into a system capable of learning from its own operation, detecting improvement opportunities, anticipating problems and sustainably raising the quality of technical response.
