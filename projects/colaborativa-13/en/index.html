From scattered incidents to SAT with memory and anticipation

Beatriz González · Thirteenth collaborative contribution to the hub on SAT digitalization, operational traceability and future use of AI to anticipate and standardize technical response

The problem

Today the Technical Assistance Service team spends a meaningful share of its time logging incidents, consolidating information from scattered channels and working with poorly structured data. That reduces useful time for incident handling, customer support and learning from previous cases.

The bottleneck is not only technological. It is operational: too much energy spent on bureaucracy and too little consolidated visibility to decide, compare and anticipate.

What this initiative proposes

The proposal follows the right sequence: first digitize capture and incident management, then structure the data and, only on top of that base, activate an AI layer able to detect recurrences, generate alerts and suggest responses.

This is not about placing AI on top of a broken process. It is about transforming SAT from a manual scheme into a system with traceability, memory and continuous-improvement capacity.

What it would add to the business

Efficiency

Less time spent on bureaucracy and manual consolidation, and more effective time on technical incident management.

Traceability

Single case record, historical view by customer and product, clear statuses and centralized documentation.

Quality

More homogeneous responses and more consistent support to customers and the commercial team.

Anticipation

Pattern detection, early alerts and progressive predictive capacity over incidents and deviations.

Main lessons

  • AI only creates real value when information is captured and normalized properly from the start.
  • The first leap is not predictive: it is leaving fragmented management and Excel as the main support behind.
  • A SAT with structured memory improves not only efficiency, but also the ability to learn from its own operation.
  • The right sequence is Manual → Digital → Intelligent, not the other way around.
Collaborative conclusion: digitizing SAT is not only about recording better. It is about building an operational base that allows better-quality responses, more time dedicated to the customer and a useful AI layer for anticipating, recommending and continuously improving the service.
