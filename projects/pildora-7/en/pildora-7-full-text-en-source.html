Pill 8. When a large company confirms you are not off track

First of all, I want to congratulate all of you on how well this initiative is being received.

The Hub has grown very quickly in pills. I have been publishing them almost as they arrived, although I have spent the last ten days traveling through Latin America and several are still waiting in the queue, which I will try to publish this weekend. But beyond that small delay, the important thing is not the publication pace. The important thing is that this is moving, that the initiative has taken hold, and that more and more of you are sharing problems, ideas, improvements, and projects with real potential.

And I encourage you to keep doing so. Really. Because this Hub only makes sense if it remains something alive, open, and useful for everyone.

A few days ago, I came across this Repsol page about their process for adopting generative AI, and I recommend that you read it calmly because it is worth it:

https://www.repsol.com/es/energia-avanzar/innovacion/adopcion-ia-generativa-repsol/index.cshtml

I read it slowly and, as I moved forward, I found myself identifying with it at many points. In some passages I even came to think, half joking and half seriously, that they had taken inspiration from us.

Obviously that is not the case. Repsol is a large multinational, with a different scale, different structure, different resources, and a much more formalized set of initiatives. But that is precisely why the reading impressed me so much: because, despite all the distance, I began to recognize there many of the things we have already started to do at Isaval.

They speak about a people-centered transformation. They speak about adoption, training, new ways of working, use cases, generative coding, responsibility, culture, and talent. In other words, they are not presenting AI as a technological fashion, but as a deep change in the way of working.

And that resonated strongly with me, because I think something similar is beginning to happen here, although in our own style.

We have not created a large corporate program or a center of competence with sophisticated names. What we have done is start working. Detect real frictions. Share them. Turn them into pills. Open conversations. Make visible opportunities that were previously scattered. And begin to build useful solutions from inside the organization itself.

There I see an important difference between Repsol and us: they tell it from an already consolidated structure; we are building it almost in real time. But at the same time, I also see our strength there. What we are doing does not come from an institutional layer. It comes from direct contact with the company's real problems.

And there is one point that, the more I think about it, the more significant it seems to me.

The Isaval systems department is made up of five people, four of whom code almost every day. And yet it has already been weeks since they wrote a single line of code in the traditional sense in which we used to understand it. And not because they have stopped building. On the contrary. They are building more than ever, but in a different way.

They have created their own ecosystems such as Isaías. They are already generating a repository of real-use applications. Some of them have been born directly from our own pill dynamic. They are enthusiastic. They are convinced that we are entering an exponential transformation in the way we solve our internal frictions. And, above all, they are seeing that this can make us a better company.

That, to me, is crucial.

Because if anyone could have seen all this as a professional threat, it was precisely them. If anyone had reasons to become defensive, it was the team closest to development and technology. And yet what they are seeing is not a threat, but a huge opportunity.

That is why reading Repsol did not create distance for me. It created confirmation.

Confirmation that we are not off track.
Confirmation that we have been fairly early in detecting where this wave was heading.
Confirmation that something serious is already happening here.
And confirmation that we should not let go of this pace.

I really encourage you to take a look at that page, because it helps to put what we are doing here into perspective. Sometimes it takes looking into a large mirror to realize that the smaller company can also be moving in the right direction.

And I, honestly, came away from that reading even more convinced than before that what we are building together at Isaval deserves to keep being pushed forward.
