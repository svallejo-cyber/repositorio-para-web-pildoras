Pill 8 · When a large company confirms you are not off track

A reading of the Repsol case as confirmation that Isaval has already activated its own early, business-grounded AI transformation.

Starting point

A recent reading on Repsol's adoption of generative AI leaves a clear impression: despite the difference in scale, many of the pieces a large multinational now formalizes are the same ones Isaval has already started to move in an intuitive, fast, and problem-driven way.

Sometimes it takes looking into a large mirror to confirm that a smaller company can also be heading in the right direction.

What Repsol is doing

Repsol does not present AI as a fashionable tool. It presents it as a people-centered transformation supported by adoption, training, new ways of working, use cases, generative coding, responsibility, and talent.

The full reference, well worth reading, is this:

Generative AI at Repsol

What we are already doing at Isaval

From the inside

Instead of starting with a formal corporate program, Isaval began by detecting real frictions and turning them into pills, conversations, and first solutions.

Business-led

The dynamic comes from direct contact with real company problems, not from a prior institutional layer.

Systems engaged

The systems team is not living this as a threat, but as an opportunity to build more and build better in a different way.

Real traction

There are already internal ecosystems such as Isaías and applications born directly from the pill dynamic.

The important comparison

Repsol tells this story from an already consolidated structure. Isaval is building it almost in real time. That difference in scale is obvious, but it does not weaken the parallel; it actually makes it more meaningful.

When a smaller organization starts moving on the same axes as a large corporation — people, adoption, use cases, governance, culture, and productivity — the reasonable conclusion is not excessive caution, but reinforcing the pace.

What this reading confirms most

Executive conclusion: the Repsol case confirms that Isaval was early in detecting where this wave was heading. Something serious, useful, and transformative is already happening here. The worst move now would be to let go of the pace.
