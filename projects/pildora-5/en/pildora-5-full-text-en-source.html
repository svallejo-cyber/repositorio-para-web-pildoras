First real AI use case at Pinturas Isaval: automation of expense-report pre-control (Tickelia)

Team,

I want to share this case because I believe it addresses a very real issue in many organizations: the growing administrative burden we accumulate around control processes.

On paper, these controls make sense. The problem starts when the effort required to feed them grows faster than the sense of actual review.

It happens with expense reports. It happens with CRM. And it happens with almost any system where we ask the organization to enter data, justify activity, or document decisions.

When that happens, a very dangerous doubt starts to appear: is anyone really reviewing all of this, or am I just filling it in because I have to?

This case came directly from a conversation with José Luis Cadelo, Northern Area Manager. I asked him to think of a real process in his day-to-day work that felt especially irritating and that he would like to automate or strengthen with intelligence.

His answer was immediate: expense review in Tickelia.

Not because it was a sophisticated process. Precisely because it was the opposite: repetitive, unrewarding, and time-consuming. Tickets, categories, supporting documents, policy checks, validations, incidents. Always the same steps.

That is where we saw a very clear opportunity: use AI not to replace managerial judgment, but to remove the mechanical part of the work.

Commercial expenses are an important line in our income statement. And they tend to grow as the company grows. Sometimes because of relaxation. Sometimes because of unclear rules. And, unfortunately, sometimes because of intentional misuse or negligence for personal benefit.

If we are not able to implement a control system that truly limits these situations, we incur two inefficiencies.

The first is failing to detect the irregularity when it occurs.

The second, and for me almost more important, is failing to generate a real sense of control. And when the organization feels that control exists only on paper, discipline relaxes.

I often compare it to Guardia Civil speed cameras on the motorway: if they did not exist, or if we believed they existed but never issued fines, we would all end up driving at 200 km/h.

When there is a real sense of control, behavior changes. People adjust more closely to the rules. The same happens with expenses.

That is why the goal of this project was not only to save review time. It was also to reinforce a control framework that is credible, consistent, and scalable.

The idea was to build a first AI-assisted review capable of reading the expense report, checking tickets and categories, comparing them against internal policy, detecting inconsistencies, and returning a structured pre-review to the manager.

The final word still belongs to the manager. AI does not decide. AI prepares, organizes, and accelerates.

To design this system, we worked with three very specific sources: the Company Expense Policy, version 09/12/2025, in force since 01/01/2026; the Tickelia app user manual; and different real expense reports exported as PDF.

From there, the validation logic was defined, the review workflow was structured, and a first prototype was built in Codex to test it with real cases.

The result is promising. We already have a first automated layer capable of reviewing, comparing, and highlighting issues before the report reaches the manager.

That means less manual checking, more consistency in policy application, and more efficient supervision.

Above all, it means starting to build systems where the organization not only has rules, but also feels that those rules are actually being reviewed.

And that changes behavior in any company.

Before taking this application to the IT department to assess how it could be incorporated into the company’s useful tools ecosystem, I would like to show it personally to anyone genuinely curious to see it working.

At the moment it runs locally on my own computer, but it already makes the potential of this kind of approach to real management processes very clear.

So, if you have made it this far and would like to have an individual conversation and a short practical demonstration, I will be very happy to show it to you.
