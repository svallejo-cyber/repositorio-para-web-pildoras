Pill 6 · AI for Tickelia expense pre-review

Sixth executive pill by Santiago Vallejo on applying AI to an internal process at Pinturas Isaval

Context

This case started in a conversation with José Luis Cadelo, Northern Area Manager. When asked to identify a real, irritating process that could be improved with intelligence, he immediately pointed to expense review in Tickelia.

It was not a sophisticated problem. It was repetitive, unrewarding, and time-consuming for management.

The operational problem

Administrative controls keep growing in every organization: expenses, CRM, supporting evidence, validations. The risk appears when the burden of feeding the system grows faster than the sense of actual review.

That creates two inefficiencies: failing to detect irregularities when they happen, and failing to create a credible sense of control.

The idea

Use AI to automate the first review of the expense report: read tickets, interpret categories, compare them with internal policy, and prepare a structured pre-review for the manager.

The goal is not to replace the manager. It is to remove the mechanical work so the manager can focus on judgment and decisions.

Why it really matters

P&L impact

Commercial expenses are a relevant line item and tend to grow as the company scales.

Real control

If the organization does not perceive actual review, behavior relaxes and rules lose force.

Useful analogy

Like motorway speed cameras: if they exist but never fine anyone, everyone eventually speeds up.

AI well applied

AI does not decide. It prepares, organizes, and accelerates supervision.

How the system was designed

  • Identification of a real friction point through a business conversation.
  • Analysis of the Company Expense Policy in force since 01/01/2026.
  • Use of the Tickelia manual and real expense reports exported as PDF.
  • Definition of validation logic and creation of a first Codex prototype.
  • Testing with real cases to verify reading, comparison, and issue detection.

First results

The system can already read reports, compare them against policy, flag inconsistencies, and generate a first structured review for the manager.

This reduces manual checking, improves consistency, and reinforces the perception of control.

Executive conclusion: we are not automating an isolated administrative task; we are building a first control layer that is more credible, more scalable, and less costly in management time.
