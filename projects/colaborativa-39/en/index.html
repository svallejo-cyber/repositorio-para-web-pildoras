Expaint Division · Isaval

Collaborative 39 · From manual price checks to intelligent price positioning

Author: Salvador Domingo
Salvador Domingo proposes structuring price setting in Expaint with an internal tool able to compare market prices, detect moves, and support pricing decisions with better commercial and margin discipline.

The current problem

Today the department needs to search item by item, review competitor websites, and run one-off checks in order to set prices. That consumes time, offers little global market visibility, and makes reaction to price changes too slow.

Less intuition, more data. Less manual checking, more continuous judgment over positioning and margin.

What impact it has

Market

Prices can drift away from the competitive reality and commercial opportunities are missed.

Margin

There is a risk of eroding margin without noticing or holding prices out of market for too long.

Decision

Decisions become less homogeneous and too dependent on isolated manual analysis.

Speed

The organization reacts late to competitor promotions, markdowns, or liquidation moves.

The opportunity

Expaint already has two valuable assets: internal price history and access to market pricing, even if that access is still manual. The proposal is to turn that combination into an internal tool able to compare, position, detect moves, and suggest prices faster.

The vision is clear: move from one-off analyses to a continuous process of competitive pricing intelligence.

How to start

The approach is realistic: choose key products, monitor two or three competitors, build a simple first model, and scale progressively. That reduces risk and makes it possible to validate quickly whether the system improves competitiveness and margin control.

Executive conclusion: this is not only about checking prices better. It is about giving the business an internal layer of continuous judgment to set prices with more control, more speed, and less dependence on scattered manual searches.
