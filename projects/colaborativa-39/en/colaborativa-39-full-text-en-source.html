Collaborative 39. From manual price checks to intelligent price positioning

Project: Price optimization and market analysis – Expaint Department

1. The problem (how we work today)

Today, to set prices, we search reference by reference, manually review competitor websites, and run occasional checks rather than continuous monitoring. This means we invest a lot of time, we do not have a global market view, and we react late to price changes.

2. The real impact

This way of working leads to prices that are poorly aligned with the market, loss of commercial opportunities, a risk of reducing margin without realizing it, and inconsistent decision-making. In short: less competitiveness and less control.

3. The opportunity

We have something very valuable: internal data (price histories) and access to market prices, even if it is still manual. But we are not using it in a structured way.

4. The solution

Create an internal price analysis tool that transforms manual work into an intelligent system, moving from manual searches and scattered information to centralized data, automatic comparison, data-based decisions, and fast reaction.

5. What will the tool do?

Compare prices. See our price against the competition in seconds.

Position us. Know whether we are expensive, in line with the market, or competitive.

Detect opportunities. Active offers, liquidations, and price drops.

Recommend prices. Suggestions based on market, margin, and history.

6. How it will be used day to day

Before setting a price, quick consultation. Periodic catalog reviews. Adjustments in response to market moves. Support in negotiations. From one-off analysis to a continuous process.

7. What we gain

Competitiveness. Margin control. Speed in decision-making. Global market visibility. Less intuition, more data.

8. How to start (realistic plan)

1. Select key products. 2. Analyze 2–3 main competitors. 3. Create a simple model (Excel). 4. Test on a small scale. 5. Scale progressively.

9. Future vision

Automation of competitor pricing. Change alerts. ERP integration. Dashboards (Power BI / dashboards).
